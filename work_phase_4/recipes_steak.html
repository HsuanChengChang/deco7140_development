Jerry’s Steak Recipe

Recipe details

Jerry’s Steak

Juicy, well-seared, and tender.

Preparation time: 25–30 min (plus resting)

A simple, restaurant-style steak that focuses on three things: seasoning, a hot sear, and proper resting. Finish with butter, garlic, and herbs for an aromatic crust.

Ingredients

  • 1 ribeye or sirloin steak (300–400g, ~2–3cm thick)
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil (canola or grapeseed)
  • Salt & freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, lightly crushed
  • Fresh thyme or rosemary (optional)

Instructions

  1. Pat the steak dry. Season generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Preheat a heavy pan (cast iron preferred) on medium-high until very hot.
  3. Add oil. Lay the steak away from you and sear 2–3 minutes per side, undisturbed.
  4. Reduce heat to medium. Add butter, garlic, and herbs; baste the steak with the melted butter for 1–2 minutes to build flavor.
  5. Check doneness (target 54–57°C / 130–135°F for medium-rare). Adjust time as needed.
  6. Transfer to a board and rest 5–7 minutes. Slice against the grain and serve.

Tips

• Bring steak to room temperature for more even cooking.
• If the pan smokes too much, lower the heat slightly and continue basting.
• Pair with a simple salad or mashed potatoes; finish with flaky salt.
