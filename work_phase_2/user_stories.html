User Stories

Food & Culinary Personas – Combined Transcript

Three personas (A: Sophia – Home Cook & Mother, B: Ahmed – International Student, C: Lily – Vegan Blogger) answer the same set of questions.

Q1: Can you describe a recent time when you tried to find recipes online but had difficulties? What made it hard?

  • Sophia: “Often I find a recipe, but the instructions aren’t clear, or there are too many ads popping up. I just give up sometimes.”
  • Ahmed: “Many recipes are only in English. I wish I could find Arabic translations.”
  • Lily: “It’s hard when sites don’t clearly label vegan recipes. I have to dig around.”

Q2: What kind of features on a food and culinary website would help you feel more connected to others?

  • Sophia: “Maybe a community for parents who share easy family recipes.”
  • Ahmed: “ Forums where international students can share tips about halal groceries or small-kitchen hacks.”
  • Lily: “I’d love a place where vegans share recipes and get feedback on their blogs.”

Q3: If you wanted to share your own recipe, cooking experience, or review, what would make it easy or difficult?

  • Sophia: “ Easy upload steps and the option to add photos.”
  • Ahmed: “I’d like to post reviews of affordable restaurants, but the website must let me filter by halal food.”
  • Lily: “I need clear categorisation — vegan, gluten-free, etc.”

Q4: How important are accessibility features such as clear steps, large images, or video demonstrations?

  • Sophia: “ Video demonstrations are a must.”
  • Ahmed: “ Clear instructions with pictures are really helpful.”
  • Lily: “Big, high-quality food photos are important for my blog style.”

Q5: What would make you trust a food community site and feel safe sharing your content?

  • Sophia: “No pop-up ads, clear privacy policies, and a respectful community would help me trust the site.”
  • Ahmed: “I’d trust a site if it’s welcoming to people from different cultures and has halal certification info.”
  • Lily: “Transparency about community rules and no stealing of content.”

Q6: Imagine you’re cooking and using the site on your phone. What problems might you face, and what features would help?

  • Sophia: “I can’t keep unlocking my phone with flour on my hands! A voice-activated option would be wonderful.”
  • Ahmed: “My student kitchen is tiny. A step-by-step mode on my phone would be easier than scrolling.”
  • Lily: “Sometimes recipe pages are too cluttered, and I can’t see images properly.”

Q7: If you could design one function that would be most useful for your community, what would it be and why?

  • Sophia: “A voice-activated cooking assistant. I could say ‘next step’ without touching the screen.”
  • Ahmed: “A halal recipe finder with bilingual support.”
  • Lily: “A smart tagging system for recipes (vegan, gluten-free, nut-free, etc.).”
