Behavioural Design

The focus of this section of the Style Guide is ensuring the user needs for usability and function are supported.

Button Styles

These buttons are designed with a clean, rounded style for clarity and ease of use. In the default state, the primary and secondary buttons use solid dark backgrounds with bold white text for strong visibility. On hover, they reverse colors, gain a black outline, and show an underline to provide clear visual feedback. When pressed (active state), the button shows an inset shadow, giving the impression of being physically pressed down. Disabled buttons use a gray palette and remove interactivity to indicate inactivity.
